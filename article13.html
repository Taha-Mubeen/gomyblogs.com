Most Expensive Watches in the World

Graff Diamonds Hallucination:

Graff Diamonds holds the record for the most expensive watch in the world with the incredible Hallucination. The Hallucination is a masterpiece worth $55 million, with over 110 carats of colored diamonds set into a platinum bracelet. Laurence Graff, Chairman of Graff Diamonds, came up with the idea for this pricey piece of Haute horology. After all, no one on the lower rungs of the corporate ladder could even consider a fifty-five-million-dollar timepiece without raising an eyebrow or two. At first glance, I was amazed and horrified that a single timepiece could cost so much, but after delving into the process by which a single rare colored diamond is sourced from a rough stone, cut to perfection, polished, and finally set into a piece of jewelry or timepiece, I realized every diamond is a story in and of itself. The Graff Diamonds Hallucination is fastened with a simple tongue clasp around the wrist. Once the bracelet is closed, the clasp is hidden and can only be unfastened by applying pressure to a single diamond.

Graff Diamonds the Fascination:

Graff Diamonds not only owns the most expensive watch ever made, but it is also the owner of the second most expensive timepiece. The watch is made up of 152.96 carats of white diamonds, with a 38.13-carat D as the center fancy-cut exquisitely rare stone. Graff cut and polished this flawless pear-shaped diamond in their workshops. This pear-shaped masterpiece can be worn as a watch dial in a highly architectural diamond bracelet or as a ring when removed. This stunning timepiece costs $40 million.

Breguet Grande Complication Marie Antoinette:

This Breguet pocket watch has an intriguing backstory. In 1783, a mystery admirer of Marie Antoinette commissioned the work, with no time or budget constraints. The only requirements were that it use gold whenever possible and have as many and varied complications as possible. It took 44 years to build and was finished 34 years after Marie Antoinette's execution and four years after Abraham-Louis Breguet died. Its incredible complexity and backstory make it an appealing purchase for affluent collectors. This incredible timepiece costs $30 million.

Jaeger-LeCoultre Joaillerie 101 Manchette:

The Jaeger-LeCoultre Joaillerie 101 Manchette, less a watch and more a piece of stunning jewelry, was given as a gift for Queen Elizabeth II's Diamond Jubilee (her 60th year of reign). This lovely bracelet combines diamond-set and polished white gold links with a miniature caliber of 101 movements. The unique design and intricate details indicate that this is a timepiece fit for a queen! This elegant timepiece is worth $26 million.

Chopard 201-Carat Watch:

The name of this incredible watch explains why it's worth $25 million. The pink diamond weighs 15 carats, the blue diamond weighs 12 carats, and the white diamond weighs 11 carats in this Chopard watch. They are set in a bracelet studded with clusters of white pear-shaped diamonds arranged in flower motifs, each with a yellow diamond protruding from the center.

Patek Philippe Henry Graves Supercomplication:

The Patek Philippe Henry Graves Supercomplication (no. 198.385) is one of the most intricate mechanical pockets watches ever made. The Supercomplication created for Henry Graves Jr. is the most expensive Patek Phillipe watch in the world. This intricate timepiece, valued at $24 million, has 24 complications, including a perpetual calendar, minute repeater with Westminster Chimes, sunrise and sunset times, and a celestial chart based on the night sky above Graves' home in New York. It was named after banker Henry Graves Jr., who allegedly commissioned it in order to outdo American automaker James Ward Packard's Grande Complication pocket watch.

Rolex Paul Newman Daytona:

Paul Newman, actor, director, race car driver, purveyor of delectable condiments, and owner of some of the world's most famous blue eyes, was the original owner of this Rolex watch. The design was first introduced in 1963 and is widely regarded as the definitive racing watch. Paul's wife, actress Joanne Woodward, gave him the watch in 1969, with the inscription "Drive Carefully" on the back. Over the next 15 years, he would wear this watch in dozens of races. As a result, Paul's undeniable cool-guy status, Hollywood history, and heartwarming story are what makes this watch so desirable. This elegant timepiece costs $18.7 million.

Jacob & Co. Billionaire Watch:

This Jacob & Co. timepiece is dubbed the Billionaire Watch for nothing. If you have $18 million to spare, you'll get a seriously luxurious watch with 260 carats of emerald-cut diamonds and a stylish skeleton face. The tourbillon's sheer beauty is enhanced by a unique skeletonized movement and magnificent architecture. Its plates have been extensively skeletonized to the point where the delicate movement is securely held in place by a web-like structure of thin bridges, all of which have been hand-refined and decorated. This keeps the focus on the quality of surface treatments and their Haute Horlogerie execution: the presence of watchmaking greatness to be appreciated, despite their exquisite surroundings.

Patek Philippe Stainless Steel Ref. 1518:

The Patek Philippe Stainless Steel Ref. 1518 was a landmark timepiece because it featured the world's first perpetual calendar and chronograph. As a result, it is a collector's item in and of itself. This watch, however, was part of a four-piece series and was made of stainless steel, which is not commonly used in luxury timepieces. These elements, when combined, make this an extremely rare watch from one of the world's leading horological houses. This one-of-a-kind timepiece costs $12 million.

Patek Philippe Ref. 1518 in Stainless Steel:

The Patek Philippe 1518, which was recently auctioned off for just over US$11 million, ranks seventh on the list of the world's most expensive watches. Because of its casing, the timepiece, which is number one in a limited edition four-piece series, is extremely rare. Unlike most Patek luxury watches, this one is made of stainless steel rather than yellow or rose gold.

Patek Philippe Gobbi Milan ‘Heures Universelles’ Ref. 2523:

The Patek Philippe Gobbi Milan' Heures Universelles' Ref. 2523 is highly sought after due to its numerous design features. For starters, only seven of this model were made in pink gold, and it's the only one in existence with a blue enamel dial signed by both the manufacturer and the retailer – Patek Philippe and Gobbi Milano. It also has a revolutionary world-time system (Heures Universelles) that truly exemplifies the design and craftsmanship of the Golden Age of watchmaking. It was made in 1953 and is all in original condition, adding to its rarity. This watch is worth $9 million.

Vacheron Constantin 57260:

The Vacheron Constantin 57260 has exceptional numbers. With 57 complications, 2,826 individual components, and 242 jewels, it is the world's most complicated watch. It also weighs 957 grams, has ten patents, took three watchmakers eight years to make, and had 85 different prototypes — all for one private collector. This watch has a Judaic calendar, a night alarm that silences the watch between 10:00 p.m. and 8:00 a.m., and a double retrograde split-seconds chronograph. It truly is a magnificent timepiece. This intricate timepiece costs $8 million.

Patek Philippe Two-Crown Worldtime Ref. 2523 Eurasia Dial:

The Patek Phillipe Two-Crown Worldtime Ref. 2523 Eurasia Dial-in yellow gold is one of only three ever made. It has a previously unseen cloisonné enamel dial with a map of Eurasia on it. This is incredible in and of itself due to the pure mastery required for this method of enameling. Other distinguishing features include the world timer, 41-city ring, and dual crowns. As a result, this watch is rightfully and accurately described as a "grail" timepiece. This timepiece is worth $7.7 million.

Patek Philippe White Gold Calibre 89:

This is one of the most complicated watches in the world, with the Calibre 89 and 33 complications. This 18-karat white gold iteration was created for Patek Philippe's 150th anniversary and is a massive timepiece. It has a diameter of 88.2 millimeters and a thickness of 41.07 millimeters. Of course, it had to be so large to accommodate all of the functionality because it has everything you could need. Sunrise and sunset timers, a second-time zone, an astronomical calendar, moon phases, a flyback chronograph, and even a thermometer are all included. This timepiece costs $6.9 million.
