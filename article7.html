Places to visit in Bangkok | Most visited city of the World

Bangkok is the capital of Thailand. It is also named “The City of Angels” or “Krun Thep” by the Thai people. It provides a range of attractions making it one of the most visited cities in the world. If you want a glimpse of Thai culture, then you’re sure to enjoy this place. It is a city of great contrast with an array of historical sites like Buddhist temples, shrines, etc next to an enthralling nightlife. You can find world-class restaurants, bustling markets, Thai massage shops, delectable cuisine, and more. If you’re stressing about which places to visit in this populous city, then look no further. We’re here to help you choose from the heaps of tourists attractions.

A visit to Bangkok would not be complete without visiting the capital’s heart and soul: the temples. These gorgeous temples are not only tourist attractions but also a place of essential Buddhist traditions. If you go early, you can see the monks performing their daily rituals, garbed in their saffron robes.

Hit two birds with one stone by visiting all these gorgeous landmarks located close to each other: The Grand Palace, Temple of the Emerald Buddha (Wat Phra Kaeo), Wat Po, and Wat Arun.

  • Grand Palace and the Emerald Buddha:

    • The Grand Palace: A sweeping, golden array of buildings and temples located at the center of Bangkok, close to the Chao Phraya river, is one of the most popular tourist destinations. It served as the residence for the royal family from its inauguration till 1925 and is now simply used for ceremonial purposes.

    The complex houses more than 100 buildings, although few places are off-limits to visitors. The complex is divided into four sections: outer, inner, and central courts along with the much-revered temple of the Emerald Buddha.

    The Outer Court used to house government departments where the involvement of the king was great. It is also where the Emeral Budhha is located. The Inner Court was reserved for the King’s royal consorts and daughters. It is still closed off to the public. The Central Court was where much of the state business was conducted. It was also the residence of the king.

    The Temple of the Emerald Buddha is located in one corner of this outer court. Out of all the ornately-adorned, golden-lacquered buildings, the Wat Phra Kaew Temple houses one of the most revered icons: the Emerald Buddha carved from a block of green jade. Every year, the Buddha’s attire is changed by the king in the summer, rainy, and winter seasons, as an official ceremony.

    Tickets are sold from 8:30 to 3:30 am and cost around 500 baht. A strict dress code must be followed owing to its sacredness. No short pants or sleeveless shirts. Modesty must be maintained.

  • Wat Pho - The Temple of the Reclining Buddha:

    • Wat Phra Chetuphon, is a 10-minute walk from the Golden Palace. It is famed for its giant 46 meters long reclining Buddha statue that is covered in gold leaf. The statue is so gigantic, that it almost feels as though it has been squeezed into the building. The buddha’s feet are decorated with exquisite mother-of-pearl illustrations. The temples also boasts of the biggest collection of Buddha statues gathered from different parts of Thailand from the ancient Ayutthaya capital to the capital of Sukhothai.

    One can also visit the chapels that enclose 394 gold-plated Buddha statues sitting in the lotus position. If you’re an appreciator of art, then look at the intricately detailed art murals. Although, deciphering their meaning can prove to be quite the task. We recommend hiring a guide to help you unveil the mysteries.

    Wait! The best part of this visit has to be discussed: The soothing traditional Thai massage!. Wat Pho is one of the leading schools of massage in Thailand. Rest assured! You lie in good hands. Take your sore shoulders there and stretch em’ out!

    As usual, the place follows a dress code so be sure to keep that in mind when you visit. For those visiting this place for the first time, a tour guide is recommended to get the best experience. They can be hired for around 200 or 400 baht.

  • Wat Arun – The Temple of Dawn:

    • This unique temple has truly passed the test of time, as it has been around since the Ayutthaya period. It is located on the Thonburi side of the Chao Phraya River. This stunning temple is exceptionally unique in its designs with ties to Hindu mythology.

    The temple consists of four small prangs and Mondops around a larger prang. The different levels of the prang are supported by mythical Yaksha demons and kinnari. It is comprised of two levels. The first level: The central Prong represents the abode of the gods: Mount Meru. It is surrounded by four Mondops which each represent the four major events of Buddha’s life (birth, first sermon, enlightenment, nirvana), and by four smaller prangs. However, since its restoration, it is off-limits to visitors. The second level has a closed entrance that is guarded by the Hindu god Indra on his three-headed elephant Erawan.

    This list of attractions is endless from the shrine that houses Buddha’s footprint to the prangs decorated with Chinese porcelain and seashells. You will also find people paying homage to Phra Prang(The god of Wind) by walking clockwise around Phra Phrang thrice, with candle pairs and three incense sticks.

  • Wat Yannawa – The Boat Temple:

    • This ancient Ayutthaya period temple is famous for its unique architecture. It was built by King Rama to emulate the Chinese Junk Ships (sailing vessels) that were getting fewer in number due to being replaced by steamships. So it serves as a remembrance. The wheelhouse contains Buddha images and many worshippers come here to give their offerings. The statue of the founder can also be found alongside the boat. One can also find a Chinese shrine dedicated to Guan Yin, as well as an ordination hall filled with Buddha images.

    This place is a definite visit, with its impressive architecture, unique history, and an abundance of heritage, religious adornments, and memorabilia. When visiting, one can buy a floral offering, as well as light incense to pay respect at the shrine.

  • Khao San Road – Famous Nightlife and Backpacker Area:

    • Looking for budget accommodations while also being able to enjoy the rich Thai culture? Look no more! The Khao San Road serves all that u need: budget accommodations, delicious traditional Thai food, and even International favorites followed by an assortment of clubs and bars to enjoy the night away.

    It is still pretty lively at daytime, but nightfall is when the real show begins. The music blares at full volume from the bars with people drinking and dancing in the streets, performers putting up an enjoyable show, and vendors tempting people with scrumptious street food and drinks. It is even more lively during the weekends. If a place does not suit your taste, then just go to another one, as each bar and club here comes with a vibe of its own. Be sure to visit the place for a night of enjoyment and revelry.

  • Chatuchak Weekend Market – Haven for shopping aficionados:

    • Traverse the Chatuchak Weekend Market, one of the biggest markets in Thailand. It is also known as the “JJ market”, and has been elevated to landmark status, is Navigate through this busy and chaotic market, and be prepared to get a complete overload of senses! You can find ANYTHING here. If by chance, there is an item that you do not find there, then it's probably not worth having. Roam around, perhaps discover some hidden markets showcasing antique goods. Bargain to your heart's content and enjoy the local life. You can find stalls on art and craft, accessories, plants, gardening tools, antique goods, ceramics, tasty food and beverages, and the list never ends. Moreover, one can visit the clock tower that was built to commemorate the 60th birthday of the late King Bhumibol Adulyadej. So shop till you drop!
