Museum of the Future | Most Beautiful Building in the World

The Museum of the Future is an exhibition area for modern and futuristic ideologies, services, and products. Located inside the Financial District of Dubai, UAE, the Museum of the Future has three essential elements: inexperienced hill, building, and void founded through the Dubai Future Foundation. It was decided to open in 2021, but as of December of that year had yet to fully do so. The Government of the United Arab Emirates has later opened the museum on 22 February of 2022. The preference of the date changed into formally made since 22 February 2022 is the Palindrome date. This Museum's mission is to encourage technological advancement and innovation, particularly in the disciplines of robots and artificial intelligence. While the museum is still under construction, it has hosted several unique exhibitions recently. The Museum of the Future was designed by architectural firm Killa Design and created by Buro Happold. It is one of the world's most difficult constructions.

Structure and Design:

It stands out for its torus shape, which resembles an oval silver sparkling ring with a large open center. In other words, the structure resembles a massive eye that sits magnificently atop the city and watches over the city's stunning landmarks and achievements. To be sure, the design has already received prestigious awards and has earned all of the points required to be rated as a LEED-certified structure. The museum is divided into three sections: the green hill, the structure, and an open or void section. Aside from architectural excellence and complexity, each of these elements is supported by an impactful theme. Because this building is round in shape there is not a single corner in this building. This building has seven floors. The outside is made of 1024 panels exactly, and each of them is covered with Arabic quotes. It is the only building in the whole world covered with a language, and it’s amazing. This is a living museum which means everything inside of it is from the present and the future, not the past. The elevator is even designed to look like a spaceship. It goes all the way up and you feel like you are in space. On the 5th floor, there is an actual NASA-approved design of a space station. It is a walk-through space that demonstrates our solar system.

On the 4th floor, there is an actual buildup of a rainforest with over 2,400 species to remind us that in the future we have to take care of nature and live with it. On the 2nd floor, there is a space dedicated to the craziest ideas from flying drones to self-driving cars and robotic dogs. And on the bottom floor, there is a full science park for future generations that helps kids to appreciate Physics, Science, and Math. This museum is not just a building it’s actually an investment fund, a university, and a lab at the same time. It is where young people can finally get support. Talented people will get funding here to pursue their craziest ideas in Math, Science, Physics, and Technology. This museum will find and fund them with millions of dollars. The future is not about buildings the future is about Science, Technology, Climate, Nature. It is about supporting humans with great ideas. It is about taking care of our planet.

Features and Facilities:

  • The Museum of the Future is not only one of the most complex museums ever constructed but also one of the most stylish.
  • National Geographic magazine has named the museum one of the world's 14 most beautiful museums.
  • Its design is impacted by the Feng Shui concept.
  • This gleaming stainless steel-clad museum stands 77 meters tall and covers an area of approximately 17,600 square meters.
  • The Arabic calligraphy (with over 700 words) engraved on its exteriors is one of the most notable features. What's more, these inspiring Arabic phrases are taken from poems written by HH Sheik Mohammed Bin Rashid Al Maktoum.
  • This structure was built using the most advanced 3D techniques.
  • It has six exhibition levels as well as an administration level, all of which are located on a three-level podium.
  • The structure's floors are the only horizontal spaces.
  • There is also an auditorium, classrooms, a theatre, and high-tech labs. There is also retail and parking.
