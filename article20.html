China's Artifical Sun

What is the “Artificial Sun”?

China’s artificial sun is making headlines for burning five times hotter than the sun and breaking world records. The “artificial sun” is a project called the Experimental Advanced Superconducting Tokamak (EAST). The operation is being carried out at a research facility in China’s Hefei city. It is called an "artificial sun" because it mimics the nuclear fusion reaction that powers the real sun.

Why it’s a breakthrough?

The entire world is chasing nuclear fusion technology and hopes to make a breakthrough soon. Researchers hope to generate an enormous amount of clean energy to power cities via nuclear fusion. China’s artificial sun has managed to beat all records by achieving a continuous high-temperature plasma operation for 102 seconds and sustaining around 119 million degrees Celsius. Previous records include: 6.5 minutes long operation time of France’s Tore Supra tokamak, 50 seconds taken by South Korea’s KSTAR whereby it maintained 50 million degrees Celsius.

What is nuclear fusion and why is it so sought after?

Nuclear Fusion is a process in which one or more light nuclei fuse to generate a single heavier nucleus. As the mass of the resulting nucleus is less than the two-parent nuclei, energy is generated from the leftover mass. This follows Einstein’s equation: E=mc2, which states that mass and energy are interchangeable.

In light of the current climate crisis, Nuclear fusion is a much more environmentally friendly option as compared to other energy-harnessing processes. As it does not emit carbon dioxide or other greenhouse gases into the environment, it does not contribute to global warming. Its sources of fuel: hydrogen and lithium are widely available. It does not create lasting radioactive nuclear waste. Fusion reactors are thought to be safe as they immediately come to a halt in case of an accident.

Gong Xianzu, one of the researchers on the project, said in a press release that as opposed to fossil fuels like coal, oil, and natural gas, which are in danger of being exhauseted and pose a threat to the environment, the artificial sun requires raw materials that are in abundance on Earth.

“Therefore, fusion energy is considered the ideal ‘ultimate energy’ for the future of humanity.”

On what principle does the Artificial Sun operate?

The sun’s enormous gravitational pressures allow nuclear fusion to occur at blazingly hot temperatures of roughly 10 million degrees Celsius. To mimic this same phenomenon at much lower pressure, the temperature must be even higher!

Here comes the tokamak an experimental device designed to harness fusion energy. The term "tokamak" is a Russian acronym that stands for "toroidal chamber with magnetic coils." It is a doughnut-shaped reactor chamber that traps super-heated gas or plasma with a powerful magnetic field. It generates power by applying powerful magnetic fields to hydrogen until it creates a searing plasma of more than 150 million degrees Celsius, and generates enormous amounts of energy when the atoms fuse. This is all done with the help of magnets and super cooling technology.

The true test has been to control the super heated plasma. The longest they have been able to control it is only two minutes. Chinese experts hope to soon extend this time with the rapid development in fusion technology. EAST is an expensive project, estimated to cost China more than $1 trillion.

The Artificial sun’s Role In ITER:

China is part of the International Thermonuclear Experimental Reactor project (ITER). This ambitious energy project sees the collaboration of 35 nations to build the world’s largest Tokamak. The members of this project are China, the European Union, India, Japan, Korea, Russia, and the United States.

The Artificial Sun will provide key support in terms of critical physics and engineering issues, to ITER in building its tokamak which is said to be the world’s largest. ITER's First Plasma is scheduled for December 2025, which will be the first act of ITER's multi-decade operational program.
