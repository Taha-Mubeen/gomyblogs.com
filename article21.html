The Great Wall of China

An ancient wall which is in China is made of cement, bricks, stones, mud, etc. It's about 196 kilometers long, 9.1 meters wide, and 15 meters high. It was meant to guard the north of China from opponent attacks and was produced in 1878. The lengthy building was made up of humans. The first section of the wall is made of compressed stone and soil. In the empire of the Ming Dynasty, the wall was made of bricks. There are 7,000 keeper halls, block houses for fighters, and beacons to transmit steam signals. There's been a rumor that people who have gone into space, said that the Great Wall of China can be seen from space because of its big presence; but this isn't real. Since the wall looks a lot like the stone and soil that encircle it, it's delicate to see with the natural eye, indeed from low Earth orbit, and it's difficult for making orbital pics.

The Great Wall of China has different sections of it as if you wanna go there, some buses can take you there which are bus line 877 or the S2 train which can take you to Badaling, and the other that drops you off at Mutianyu with a transfer at Huariou is the bus express (916). If you want to have a good experience and want to know everything about its history, it'll cost you around $30 per person, which includes a mini-transportation bus with an English-speaking guide and a driver of the bus. Don't visit The Great Wall of China at weekends or on holidays, because at that time it's very crowded. Keep in mind that it isn't only for foreigners, but the Chinese also come to see it on their holidays. The time of visiting this place is 09:00 am to 04:30 pm.

Shanghai Tower

Shanghai Tower is the tallest building in China, having 128-stories and is 632-meter-tall. It's located in Pudong, Shanghai. It is the second tallest building in the world. Since 2015, it's been the tallest LEED Platinum-certified building in the world. With a building of 562 m, it shares the record of having the world's largest observation deck. Until 2017, with a speed of 20.5 meters, it had the second-fastest elevators in the world. It is the tallest building in Pudong city. It has nine separate zones which are divided into offices, retail, and leisure use. The Shanghai Tower is owned by Yeti Construction and Development.

In November 2008 construction work began on this tower and on 3 August 2013 it was completed. In 2015 summer its exterior was completed and in September 2015 it was completed. In November 2014 it was scheduled to open the tower to the public, but it was shifted to February 2015. The viewing point was opened to the public or visitors in July 2016. On April 26, 2017, the viewing point was opened to the public, which is on the 118th floor of it. There are also two super tall skyscrapers standing right next to it. To go into Shanghai Tower costs a ticket which is $26(180 Chinese Yuan). The time of visiting this place is 8:30 am to 9:30 pm.

Li River

The Li River or Li Jiang is the name for the upper level of the Gui River in northwestern, China. It's a piece of the Xijiang River network in the Pearl River Basin. The stream flows 164 kilometers from Xi'an to Pingle country, where the karst peaks and stream views highlight the visible Li River cruise. The Li River originates in Mao's peaks in Xing' a County and flows in the overall southern direction through Guilin, Yangshuo, and Pingle. The Li River merges with the Lipu River and the Gui River continues to the south, which at last falls into the Xi Jiang, in Wuzhou. Tourists riding boat cruises from Xingping on the Li River. The upper line of the Li River is joined by the aged Lingqu Canal with the Xiang River, which flows north into the Yangtze; this in history made the Li and Gui Rivers piece of a large waterway joining the Yangtze Valley with the Pearl River Delta.

The 439- kilometer procedure of the Li and Gui Rivers is adjoined by green hills. Cormorant fishing is much associated with the Lijiang. Li River sailings from Guilin to Yangshuo are famous, attracting millions of visitors a year. The ticket for getting into Li River is of two categories adults and children. The price of tickets for adults is $139 per adult and for children is $75 per child. Summer Palace

In Beijing, there's a vast ensemble of gardens, lobbies, and lakes known as Summer Palace. It's also known as the "Imperial Garden Museum". It was built in 1750 by Emperor Qianlong. It was a grand lobby in the Qing dynasty. It covers a length of 2.9 square kilometers. Longevity Hill is around 60 meters tall and has numerous structures laid in sequence. The front hill is rich with epic lobbies and summerhouses, while the back hill, in edgy unlikeness, is hidden with natural beauty. The mid-Kunming Lake, crossing 2.2 square kilometers, was entirely manufactured and the shoveled soil was used to construct Longevity Hill. Inspired by the gardens in South China, in the Summer Palace, there are over 3,000 colorful Chinese ancient structures that put up a library of over 40,000 types of precious historical bones from each dynasty.

The Forbidden City and Imperial Palace

China's largest and most historic structure, the Outlawed City( Zijincheng) – also known as the Imperial Palace is located in the very heart of Beijing and is a must-see when visiting the country. Starting during the Yuan Dynasty between 1271- 1368, largely of the complex scene was made between 1406 and 1420. Many epic palaces in one, this big establishment was the home of 24 Ming and Qing Emperors, whose presence banned the entry of anyone other than the epic family and their wives. Covering some 720,000 square meters and guarded by a 10-meter-high wall with watchtowers and a broad drain, this heavy establishment consists of areas set aside for regular and executive things, as well as a private home used by the emperor.

While it can hold multiple hours to know everything, highlights involve the five white marble Golden River Bridges; the Hall of Supreme Harmony, a 35-meter-high structure housing the epic throne; the delicate emperor's inner lobby and the Palace Museum with its big library of art and museum pieces from the Ming and Qing dynasties. Other important attractions near the Imperial Palace include the well-known Tiananmen Square, and the Temple of Heaven, one of the country's most historic religious points, which dates to the 15th century.

The opening and closing times of it are from 1st April to 31st October; its timing is 8:30 - 17:00, and from 1st November to 31st March its timing is 8:30 - 16:30.
