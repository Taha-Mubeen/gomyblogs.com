Narcissus-The Self Lover | Greek Mythology

Greek mythology, a genre of Ancient Greek folklore, has always been retold and enjoyed by all with its stories on their various gods, goddesses, heroes, etc, and all their cruel yet thrilling antics. One such story is of Narcissus: The self-lover.

Narcissus was born through the union of Liriope, a nymph, and the river god, Cephissus. Narcissus was a man of extraordinary beauty and left many yearning for him. When he was young, his mother went to a seer named Tiresias. As soon as Tiresias saw Narcissus, he immediately knew of the terrible fate that would befall him. He warned his mother: “Oh Liriope, Mother of Narcissus, I fear your son may not live long if he recognizes himself.”

His mother immediately understood the meaning of his words. As soon as she reached home, she made sure to get rid of the mirrors in the house. As Narcissus grew older, his beauty refined even further, and the trail of broken-hearted maidens grew ever so long. Yet, no one could tug at his heartstrings. Not until he saw his reflection in the mirror and finally recognized his allure. He became enamored with himself but this relationship was bound to fail. How can one expect a response from oneself? Narcissus, distraught, pined away in despair by the reflection till he ultimately died of hunger and thirst. His corpse bore flowers, which then bore his name: Narcissus!

Narcissus and Echo:

The next most widely known version of this story comes from “The Metamorphoses,” a narrative poem written by the Roman poet Ovid. This version of the story features Echo, a mountain nymph who was cursed by Juno (Hera in Greek Mythology). Her husband, Jupiter (Zeus in Greek Mythology), would often consort with beautiful nymphs and visit Earth for this purpose. Juno soon became suspicious of his visits and in an attempt to catch him red-handed, went after him. However, each time Juno would miss Jupiter because of Echo’s long-drawn-out conversations. She soon realized that this was but a ploy to save him and give him time to run away. She cursed Echo to only repeat the last words that she heard.

Soon Echo came across the beautiful youth Narcissus while he was out hunting with his friends. She immediately fell in love with him. Due to the curse inflicted upon her, she could not call out to him and chose to quietly observe him. However, her longing for him grew day by day.

One day Narcissus got separated from his hunting companions. He called out to them but to no avail. Suddenly, he heard Echo repeat his words:”Anyone here?” Upon which he answered,” Come here.” Echo, who had become infatuated with him, rushed to Narcissus and embraced him but was coldly rejected. Ironically, her love for Narcissus only grew.

The tale then follows the same path. Narcissus looked at his reflection and fell in love with himself. He then withered away, upon realizing that his love could not be materialized. When Narcissuses died, Echo mourned for him till she was but ashes. Today, all that remains of Echo is her…… sound.

It is also believed that Nemesis, the goddess of retribution, learned of his cruel rejection and decided to lure him towards a pool of water so that he may recognize himself and ultimately be doomed.

Other Versions

There are many versions to this particular story and each ends in the same painful way.

  • The Greek traveler, Pausanias, also wrote a version of the story where Narcissus had a twin sister whom he loved very much. They spent much of their time together. Unfortunately, his sister died. As they were both twins, Narcissus would occasionally go by the riverside to look at his reflection and ease the pain of losing his sister.
  • In Conon’s version of the story, Narcissus rejects a very persistent admirer named “Ameinias” by sending him a sword and indirectly telling him to kill himself. Armeinias comes to his doorsteps, cursing him for all that pain that he caused, and kills himself right then and there. The story then follows with Narcissus becoming infatuated with himself and committing suicide. Though, in this version, it is said that Narcissus did feel remorse.
